แวะมาดู product Lines ของ Apple เล่นๆ

1984

On the TV broadcast of Super Bowl XVIII, Apple debuts the first Macintosh PC with one of the most famous TV commercials in history.  A year later, co-founder Steve Jobs is  forced out in a boardroom coup.

1989

The first Macintosh Portable is launched. Sales weren’t great and the product was discontinued in 1991.

1991

Apple releases the PowerBook, arguably the first modern portable laptop computer. The PowerBook would survive in various forms for 15 years until being replaced by the MacBook Pro.

1993

Macintosh TV. Apple introduces a variation of its Macintosh Performa that could switch from being a computer display to a cable-ready television. It may have been ahead of its time, as only 10,000 were made and it was discontinued less than six months after its introduction.

1993-1994

Apple unveils two of its loudest and quietest failures: The Newton, one of the first PDA devices, and the QuickTake, one of the earliest consumer digital cameras. 

1995

The Pippin, Apple’s foray into the videogame console market, makes its debut. .

1997

Jobs is named interim CEO, less than a year after returning to Apple.

1999

The iMac is introduced. Apple did away with the floppy disk drive in favor of a front-loading CD drive. The iMac has since undergone three major redesigns, and the current flat-screen version is the most popular Mac ever.

2001

The first iPod is released. Five years and several upgrades and redesigns later, more than 100 million have been sold.

March 21, 2007

Apple TV begins shipping. Apple sets out to conquer the living room with a device that can wirelessly stream to a widescreen television music, photos and video from a Mac or PC that has Apple’s iTunes Music Store.

June 29, 2007

The iPhone goes on sale.
