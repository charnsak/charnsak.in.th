City center:
- kim kocht to go @ merkur hoher markt: pretty famous haute cuisine chef cooks at a supermarket lunch to go
- minarittenstüberl: famous TV chef cooks Austrian lunch in a cantine
- palatschinkenkuchl @ köllnerhofgasse: Austrian crêpes not very expensive for 1st district
- reinthaler's beisl @ dorotheergasse 4: Austrian food especially wiener schnitzel good and not very expensive in 1st district - reservation necessary!
- coffee dorotheum @ dorotheergasse 17: in the building of the auction house, there is a nice coffee shop with a really nice view
- da capo @ schulerstraße 18: good and nice Italian restaurant, try out the first floor and not the basement. Reservation needed, especially in the evening
- esterhazykeller @ haarhof: very very old building, traditional food. Worth a visit. Reservation needed, especially in the evening
- bockshorn @ naglergasse 7: smallest pub in Vienna
- Haas & Haas @ stephansplatz 4: nice tee shop with coffee bar where you can sit and trink tee/coffee. If you need to warm up in winter this is the place
- Gutenberg & Fieglmüller: you already know
- MAK @ Stubenring 5: Museum of Applied & Contemporary Art - food is very expensive, but a coffee should be nice 
- cafe schottenstift @ freyung 6a: if you're too cold after the Christmas market at rathaus or freyung, you can go there for a coffee (maybe grumpy waiters if authentic ;-))
- wunderbar @ schönlaterngasse: tiny bar, but authentic
- cafe schwarzenberg @ schwarzenbergplatz: authentic with grumpy waiters (very traditional!)

6th district:
- café sperl: authentic coffee
- neni am naschmarkt: oriental food, mostly Israeli kitchen, very very very good caramelized eggplants with chili and ginger
- zur eisenernen zeit: also at naschmarkt like neni, but authentic Austrian dishes
- tofu & chili @ linke wienzeile 18: tiny Asian restaurant. Good, cheap, fresh
- natsu @ burggasse or gumpendorfer straße: www.natsu-sushi.at - you have to order the half price offers. Cheapest and one of the best sushi places in Vienna. Tiny.
- ra'mien: I love the ramen there
- mill: one of the best Sunday brunch places in town, but reservation required. Monday to Saturday you can also go there if the weather is good, since the backyard is very nice to sit outside
- Phil: coffee and book/music store. Its like a second living room with readings sometimes
- MQ: bars in museumsquartier are famous for locals as well. in winter you can get also a punsch at the glacis beisl

7th district:
- brunch @ KHM: http://www.khm.at/en/explore/angebote/cafe-restaurant exclusive brunch in the museum in the gallery - I've heard that its pretty cool since you can walk around and look at the pictures in between
- coffee @ kaufhaus Leiner: this is a furniture store but it has a coffee/diner in the rooftop floor with a really nice view at the beginning of mariahilfer straße
- siebensternbräu: micro-brewery with homemade beer and authentic food

Others:
- coffee kunsthaus @ untere weißgerberstr 13: friedensreich hundertwasser museum and house
- chinarestaurant no. 27 @ ungargasse: authentic Chinese. Fancy but at the end of the menu you find the good stuff 
- Rudolf Ölz: bakery at landstraße
- süssi: tiny chocolate place and French tea House with 3 tables to sit down
- cha no ma: Japanese green tea place. Incredibly good macha smoothies and Onigiri
- zur böhmische kuchl: restaurant in the 8th district famous for its deserts
- cafe eiles: students place. Authentic Viennese.
- heuriger schübel-auer @ nußdorf: beautiful traditional tavern almost outside of town. closed between Christmas and February
- schreiberhaus @ neustift am walde: same issue here, but also a "Heuriger" (tavern), which are very famous here
- modul restaurant cottage: far away in the 19th district with nice view over Vienna
- gloriette cafe: if you visit schönbrunn you can stop by. Nice place.
- tirolergarten @ tiergarten schönbrunn: if you don't enter via the zoo you don't have to pay the entry fee. But you can also rest there when you visit the zoo...
